# StudyOS Product Brief

## Register

Product.

## Product Purpose

StudyOS helps students turn class materials, deadlines, and availability into a realistic study plan. The product should reduce planning overhead, surface the next useful action, and keep students focused on studying instead of configuring a system.

## Primary Users

Students managing multiple subjects, uploaded PDFs, quizzes, flashcards, deadlines, and study sessions. They may be using the app between classes, at a desk before a study block, or on mobile when checking what comes next.

## Core Jobs

- Create subjects and attach study materials.
- Add deadlines and availability.
- Generate a feasible plan from prerequisites.
- Complete, miss, skip, or reschedule sessions.
- Practice from uploaded files with quizzes and flashcards.
- Export plans and summaries when needed.

## Strategic Principles

- The app should always answer: what should I do next?
- Planning prerequisites belong near plan generation when possible.
- Avoid sending users across pages to finish a single task.
- Prefer inline forms, guided setup, and state-aware actions over generic links.
- Keep destructive actions clear, accessible, and reversible only when actually supported.
- Empty states should explain the blocker and provide the exact next action.

## Tone

Clear, calm, and practical. Copy should be direct and brief. Avoid motivational filler, vague productivity language, and repeated headings.

## Anti-References

- Generic dashboard grids where every card has equal importance.
- Setup flows that require users to remember prerequisites across multiple pages.
- Icon-only controls without accessible labels.
- Browser alerts for normal validation or recoverable errors.
- Decorative UI that slows down the core study workflow.

## Success Criteria

- A new user can reach plan generation from the Plan page without leaving it.
- Returning users can identify and act on the next study task from the dashboard.
- Keyboard and screen-reader users can operate primary flows without hidden labels or hover-only affordances.
- Common flows require fewer clicks than separate-page setup.
